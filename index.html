The Congo connection

Most of this cobalt traces back to the DRC.

About 76% of the world's cobalt is mined in the Democratic Republic of Congo. Control over those mines funds armed groups and fuels a decades-long conflict. The cobalt in your battery is part of that demand.

What you can do

Keep it longer. Repair it. Recycle it.

Every extra year a phone stays in use means less pressure to mine new cobalt. Repair before replacing, and recycle old devices so their cobalt re-enters the supply chain instead of new mines.

Estimate basis

Cobalt is estimated from battery capacity. Congo exposure, labour, wage and worker-day figures are awareness proxies — not claims about a specific brand or model.
