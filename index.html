Poster context

Cobalt, Congo, and conflict minerals

Most mined cobalt comes from the Democratic Republic of Congo. Industrial mines, artisanal mining, traders, refiners, battery makers, and phone brands can all sit inside one long supply chain. This checker turns a phone battery into a simple exhibit estimate, not a brand-specific audit.

What students can take away

Keep phones longer

Every extra year reduces demand for newly mined battery minerals.

Repair before replacing

Battery replacement and screen repair can lower the pressure for new devices.

Recycle responsibly

Old phones contain recoverable metals, but they only help if they enter a real recycling stream.

Estimation basis

Cobalt uses battery capacity and a 0.45-0.85g cobalt per Wh range. DRC exposure uses a 76% global mined-cobalt share. Labour and wage pressure are risk-weighted proxies using a 20% artisanal-mining share, 4 worker-minutes per gram of artisanal-risk cobalt, and a low-wage range of $1-$2 per day. Battery CO2e uses 58-92kg CO2e per kWh of lithium-ion cell production.
